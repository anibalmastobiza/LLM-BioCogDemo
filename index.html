LLM-BioCogDemo

Investigating the performance of AI language models in bioethics, cognitive science, and democratic deliberation

Project Summary

This project explores the capability of AI language models to perform tasks in bioethics, cognitive science, and democratic deliberation, evaluating their validity and reliability.

Objectives

  • Develop a rigorous methodology to evaluate language models in the domains of cognitive science, bioethics, and democratic deliberation.
  • Explore whether the models can act as proxies for human cognition under certain conditions.
  • Establish best practices for evaluating language models.

Methodology

The project employs an iterative methodology to analyze the performance of language models across three domains: cognitive science, bioethics, and democratic deliberation, combining experimental and qualitative methods.

Work Packages

  • WP1: Evaluation of the cognitive capabilities of language models.
  • WP2: Exploration of the use of language models in bioethics.
  • WP3: Study of the role of models in democratic deliberation.
  • WP4: Ethical analysis of language models in practical scenarios.

Dissemination Plan

The project will be disseminated through academic journal publications, conference presentations, and activities on social media and public platforms, aiming to reach both the scientific community and the general public.
