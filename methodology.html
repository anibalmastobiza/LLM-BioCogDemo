Research Methodology

A comprehensive framework for evaluating language model capabilities

Methodological Approach

The LLM-BioCogDemo project employs a mixed-methods approach combining experimental and qualitative research techniques to evaluate language models across three domains. Our methodology ensures rigorous assessment while maintaining contextual understanding of model outputs.

Research Phases

01

Task Design & Development

In this initial phase, we develop domain-specific tasks that probe different aspects of cognitive, ethical, and deliberative capabilities:

  • Cognitive tasks targeting inferential reasoning, conceptual understanding, and problem-solving
  • Bioethical scenarios requiring normative reasoning and ethical framework application
  • Deliberative exercises assessing facilitation, summarization, and consensus-building

All tasks undergo validation by domain experts before implementation.

Task Matrix

Mapped across dimensions of complexity, domain knowledge, and reasoning type

02

Data Collection

Our data collection process involves parallel assessment of language models and human participants:

  • Language model outputs are collected using standardized prompts across multiple model versions
  • Human responses are gathered from demographically diverse participants with varying levels of domain expertise
  • All responses are anonymized and coded for analysis

Collection Framework

Systematic approach ensuring comparable data between models and humans

03

Analysis Framework

Our multi-layered analysis approach combines:

  • Quantitative evaluation using established performance metrics
  • Qualitative content analysis to identify reasoning patterns and approaches
  • Comparative assessment between model and human responses
  • Expert panel review of subset of responses

Multi-Dimensional Analysis

Combining metrics for comprehensive capability assessment

Evaluation Metrics

Cognitive Performance

  • Accuracy on established cognitive tests
  • Consistency across related problems
  • Error pattern analysis
  • Novel solution generation

Ethical Reasoning

  • Framework consistency
  • Principle application
  • Contextual sensitivity
  • Justification quality

Deliberative Capacity

  • Perspective incorporation
  • Argument quality
  • Facilitation effectiveness
  • Fairness assessment

Validation Approaches

To ensure robustness of our findings, we employ multiple validation strategies:

Cross-Validation

We use k-fold cross-validation to ensure findings generalize across different subsets of our tasks.

Expert Consensus

Domain experts independently evaluate response quality and provide consensus judgments.

Adversarial Testing

We subject models to adversarial examples designed to probe the boundaries of their capabilities.
