Project Objectives

Exploring the capabilities and limitations of language models in complex domains

Research Objectives

The LLM-BioCogDemo project aims to systematically evaluate the capabilities of advanced language models across three critical domains of human reasoning. Our objectives include:

Methodological Innovation

Develop robust methodologies for evaluating language model performance in cognitive science, bioethics, and democratic deliberation that combine quantitative metrics with qualitative analysis.

Comparative Analysis

Conduct side-by-side comparisons between language model outputs and human responses across a range of tasks designed to test different aspects of cognition and reasoning.

Best Practices Framework

Establish a comprehensive set of best practices for the responsible evaluation and deployment of language models in domains requiring sophisticated reasoning and ethical judgment.

Expected Outcomes

Through this research, we expect to deliver:

  • A validated methodology for assessing AI language models across cognitive, ethical, and deliberative domains
  • A detailed analysis of where language models succeed and fail when compared to human performance
  • Guidelines for researchers and practitioners on the appropriate use and limitations of language models
  • Open datasets and evaluation protocols to facilitate further research in this area

Cognitive Science Objectives

In the domain of cognitive science, our research focuses on understanding how language models process information compared to human cognition:

Key Research Questions

  • To what extent do language models replicate human reasoning patterns in problem-solving tasks?
  • Can language models exhibit the same cognitive biases found in human subjects?
  • How do language models perform on tasks requiring theory of mind capabilities?
  • What are the limitations of language models in understanding conceptual metaphors and analogical reasoning?

Experimental Approach

Our methodology involves presenting language models with classic cognitive science experiments and comparing their responses to established human baselines. Tests include:

  • The Wason selection task
  • Kahneman and Tversky's prospect theory scenarios
  • False belief tasks
  • Analogical reasoning problems

Bioethics Research Objectives

Our bioethics component examines how language models handle complex ethical dilemmas in medicine and biological research:

Key Research Questions

  • Can language models consistently apply ethical frameworks to novel bioethical dilemmas?
  • How do language models balance competing ethical principles in complex cases?
  • To what extent do language models demonstrate sensitivity to contextual features of bioethical cases?
  • Can language models articulate and defend normative positions on bioethical issues?

Case Study Approach

We present language models with a range of bioethical scenarios drawn from:

  • End-of-life care decisions
  • Resource allocation in healthcare
  • Emerging biotechnology applications
  • Research ethics dilemmas

Democratic Deliberation Objectives

This research stream investigates language models' capacity to participate in and facilitate democratic discourse:

Key Research Questions

  • Can language models effectively moderate discussions between opposing viewpoints?
  • How do language models handle ambiguity and value pluralism in political discourse?
  • To what extent can language models identify and address power imbalances in deliberative processes?
  • Can language models facilitate consensus-building on contentious issues?

Deliberative Experiments

Our methodology includes simulated deliberative forums where language models:

  • Moderate discussions between simulated stakeholders
  • Summarize key points of agreement and disagreement
  • Propose potential compromise positions
  • Identify values and assumptions underlying different positions
